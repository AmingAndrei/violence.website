TARTARUS is an Undercity and autonomous system serving as the penal complex of the Chromatican City.

Structure

The complex occupies a surface area of ~225 km², forming a square megastructure measuring 15 km on each side. Architecturally, TARTARUS resembles a densely layered residential block complex, with design influences comparable to utilitarian mass-housing developments. The entire facility is constructed from reinforced concrete. Interior spaces are organised into numerous apartment-like units, each containing only basic sanitation infrastructure. No doors, glass windows, or conventional furnishings are present within the structure. Illumination is provided exclusively by ceiling-mounted floodlights, which operate on a programmed day–night cycle. Entry and exit are restricted to access points located in the ceiling.

Living Conditions

Inmates are permitted to move freely throughout the complex, though certain areas may be difficult or hazardous to access due to unstable architectural elements or hostile environmental conditions. Essential supplies, including food, clothing, and requested basic items such as mattresses, are distributed weekly. These provisions are deposited at randomised locations, with a tendency toward areas of higher population density.

TARTARUS System

At fixed intervals of seven days, the TARTARUS System, originally designed with keeping inmates in and intruders out, activates an automated protocol lasting approximately eight hours. During this period, specialised machine units are deployed throughout the structure with the explicit objective of locating and eliminating prisoners. These machines are engineered for superior maneuverability within the facility’s complex spatial layout. Simultaneously, Voxel-based structural mechanisms initiate large-scale architectural reconfiguration. These changes range from gradual adjustments to abrupt transformations and are not always survivable. Upon completion of this cycle, the units withdraw to undisclosed internal storage or standby locations, after which the scheduled supply distribution phase begins. Deceased individuals are systematically recovered by automated processes. The facility’s system is largely self-governing, with direct human involvement typically limited to intake and release procedures as well as routine structural inspections.
