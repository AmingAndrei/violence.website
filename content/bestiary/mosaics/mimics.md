Mimics (_Mosaica mosaica mimisis_) are a species of copycat Mosaics.
# .anatomy
<div class="bestiary-divider"></div>

In their base form, Mimics have a gangly, roughly humanoid appearance, reaching a height of 2 m. Their bodies are cyan to light blue surface. Their faces are smooth, their only feature being a pair of glowing eyes.
# .behaviour
<div class="bestiary-divider"></div>

Mimics are always aggressive and will attack unprovoked. They were created by Violence Against Art as crawler drones, designed observe and catalogue all the information about surrounding creatures and individuals, which is later used in the creation of Mannequins. They have no higher thought or individuality, and have an extremely limited intelligence, working entirely within their programming.
# .abilities
<div class="bestiary-divider"></div>

Mimics have the ability to... mimic... other beings. Their bodies can shapeshift into resembling any being, species or individual it has observed. These mimicries are extremely detailed, as they will analyse even the most minute and imperceptible behaviours and imitate them perfectly, even including things such as speech patterns. They can switch between mimicry profiles on the fly.

The ability falls short in some aspects. While they can change their body plan, its surface cannot and will remain a cyan-blue crystal. And due to their extremely faithful imitation, they will reproduce the flaws and weaknesses of whatever they're currently mimicking, even if the Mimic itself has none of the flaws and weaknesses. They cannot combine profiles or pick-and-chose what to mimic from a profile, they need to load profiles in their entirety. They also cannot copy memories, only working with the given information and extrapolate outwards, which means they will more often than not hallucinate information when asked about things it didn't witness or that it has a limited understanding off. And since they switch profiles to the most convenient form for a situation, they can be lured into assuming a weaker form that can be taken advantage of.
# .reproduction
<div class="bestiary-divider"></div>

Mimics reproduce through mitosis. Whenever an individual faces a situation that puts its life in overwhelming danger, it will revert to its base form, and its cells will begin rapidly vibrating laterally. These vibrations will create two distinct after-images, with the cells rapidly splitting to occupy their space, the two after-images eventually separating completely from one another and forming two individuals. The two new Mimics retain all of the memories of the old one, but unless refreshed, these memories can accumulate errors between transfers.
