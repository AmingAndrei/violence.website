A Guest is a humanoid construct categorised as a form of organic technology, created through the artificial reanimation of a deceased human body.

Creation

The creation of a Guest requires a clinically dead human body, specifically one that has undergone irreversible cessation of all neurological and cardiac activity. The transformation process involves extensive biological reconstruction and augmentation, including but not limited to surgical grafting of artificial and biological components, reconstruction or replacement of nervous and skeletal systems, and installation of internal life-support and sensory rerouting modules.

A specialised helmet is permanently affixed to the cranium of the corpse, mechanically integrated and fused with the skull. The helmet includes an LED-based animated facial display, environmental filtration systems, enabling operation in toxic atmospheres, underwater environments, and complete vacuum, oxygen storage and recycling units and integrated radio communication systems.

Over time, the biological contents of the skull undergo advanced decomposition. All solid and structural tissue is eventually broken down, replaced by a homogeneous organic slurry retained within the helmet’s sealed interior. Despite this, external sight and hearing are preserved via artificial input: visual signals are routed through the visor and aligned to the facial display, while audio input is managed through external microphones. The destruction of the visor results in instantaneous functional termination of the Guest.

Neurological Function

The Guest is "activated" through the forced reactivation of dormant neural pathways. Certain low-level neural patterns such as basic language processing and locomotion may be retained or partially reconstructed. However, memory, personality, and complex cognitive traits are absent or irretrievably lost. In many cases, further degeneration of nerve cells post-mortem introduces additional dysfunction, resulting in a wide spectrum of neurological impairment ranging from negligible to critically debilitating. If the host body retains any active neural activity at the time of the procedure, the process will trigger fatal seizures.

Limits

Guests possess structural adaptations enabling entry into BLACKSPACE, where they exhibit full resistance to Coherence degradation effects for a maximum of 3 hours. Beyond this window, immunity fails.

Due to their modular construction, Guests are capable of internal organ and external limb replacement, provided the correct biological interfaces are maintained. Additionally, their systems require periodic blood transfusions every ~24 hours, to prevent sepsis and multi-system failure.

Not all human corpses are viable candidates for Guest conversion. Disqualifying conditions include severe tissue necrosis or putrefaction, major structural deformation or dismemberment, or the corpse belonging to a Redliner, due to systemic post-mortem disintegration of the peripheral nervous system.
