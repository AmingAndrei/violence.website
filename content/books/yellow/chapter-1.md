<h1 style="text-align:center;font-size:2.5rem;letter-spacing:0.2em;margin:0 0 2rem;">Chapter 1: Peachy</h1>

<p><strong>10.03.10 AW, 07:59, CHROMATICAN CITY</strong></p>

<p>Quiet. Only the howling winds outside the city and the crackling electricity filled the silence. Then the clock struck 8. The streetlight dimmed ever so slightly as power surged, illuminating the streets in artificial daylight. And the city awoke, obsidian skyscrapers as long as the eye could see, searchlights darting all around the dark sky. This was the Chromatican City, what came to be known as the last stronghold of human civilization.</p>

<p>In it's very heart stood a spire, mere sheets and chunks of metal grafted upon each other, reaching far above the clouds as if to hold the firmament itself. The Spine, and within it sat the Pale, the new powers that be.</p>

<p>But good things don't last. The emergency broadcast screamed, warnings for the citizens to evacuate the area now! The searchlights homed in on the approaching threat, its silhouette now visible in front of the black clouds.</p>

<p>The beast landed in the middle of a large boulevard, the concrete cracking under its immense weight. A large, draconic being, metal scales shimmering in the light, nearly 30 meters from head to tail. Its roar deafened all who dared be near it, stomping across all that stood in its proximity.</p>

<p>And high above, a single helicopter circled the area, surveying the situation. Its large door opened, and from within dangled a man, loosely holding himself in with one hand.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“I’ve got visuals on the Zmey. How did it even get this close?"</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>"One of my Signal Station technicians 'forgot' about his broken air filter and croaked in his sleep,"</i> responded a woman on a radio, tired and monotonous.</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">"That's a shame. I should inform you right now I'm declining the spot."</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>"Don't worry, Jacques. You're both too valuable for that. And I'd hazard a guess that you can handle the situation?"</i></p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Count on us.”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“Good. I've placed the relocation cage on a nearby street. Keep me posted.”</i></p></blockquote>

<p>The man appeared to be pushing thirty, clean-shaven, hair not particularly styled but obviously well taken care of. The clothes he wore were nothing to write home about, with the only exception being the surprisingly stylish coat that was part of his uniform. From his belt hung a small pouch, a slight glow emanating from within whenever a ray of light passed over the opening. But most striking was the massive slab of metal strapped to his back, an axe too big to be used as a weapon. It's looks suggested it once served as a motorcycle's engine, but that couldn't be further from the truth.</p>

<p>Jacques pulled out a bottle of gasoline from his many pockets and poured it directly on the axe's surface, the pilot's face turning into a scowl from the smell.</p>

<p class="Human">“Alright, let’s do this!” he muttered, pulling a small earpiece from his other pocket and turning it on.</p>

<p>And then immediately yanking it from his eardrum's vicinity. The sound coming out was louder than the rotor blades.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Okay, listen, I- No, you- I can't understand you when you- NO, YOU LISTEN- Will you let me just-”</p></blockquote>

<p>But to no avail. The man was stuck arguing with the buzzing noise on the other end, the axe on his back braking and clanking. Until...</p><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“Jacques,”</i> the radio woman interrupted, much more impatient. <i>“I can see the Zmey destroying the city's infrastructure. What I can't see is you. Why is that?"</i></p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Velos.”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“Put the on the same channel.”</i></p></blockquote>

<p>Jacques pressed a few buttons, and both receivers went silent. A moment passed, and the axe began revving, sparks flying out of it's chassis. The helicopter lowered itself just enough for Jacques to leap onto a rooftop without breaking his legs.</p>

<p>He glanced over at the situation down below. Several people hid inside a café, several hid behind their cars, several within a bus, wherever they possibly could on such short notice. The Zmey wasn't actively targeting people, more so curious about it's surroundings, but it was still a wild animal. If it were to get startled…</p>

<p>He didn’t have any time to waste or to take the lift. Jacques jumped off the rooftop, swinging the axe's blade into a wall, gliding to the ground. He whistled, drawing the beast's attention.</p>

<p>The Zmey turned around and roared, batting its wings, trying to make itself seem imposing. Jacques casually approached it. He reached over his shoulder, grabbing the axe's handle, revving the engine inside the mass of metal, roaring in return.</p>

<p>The creature flinched at the sound and, startled, in one deep breath, unleashed a stream of yellow fire towards the man. He ran behind the rubble caused by the beast’s landing, waiting out the Zmey's lung capacity.</p>

<p>As soon as it was done, Jacques hurried towards a maintenance hatch covering the city’s water lines. He pulled the axe, its metal plates shifting and melting into the form of a sword, plunging it into the ground.</p>

<p>A jet of water burst upward, drenching the air and throwing the Zmey off balance. Jacques darted in close. Whatever drops of water touched the dragon’s scales turned to steam as soon as they made contact. With the corner of his eye he could see it, the relocation cage he was supposed to guide the Zmey towards. And in his mind, Jacques formulated an idea.</p>

<p>Jacques ran to the creature's legs, six in total, their appearance more similar to that of an insect than to a reptile. Except for the claws, those were surely reptilian.</p>

<p>He swung the axe. Or rather, the axe swung itself, with Jacques being there for support.</p>

<p>He plunged the blade into one of the Zmey’s leg joints. He forced the segments apart until the cartilage gave way with a wet, squelching pop, the joint pulling apart in a clean, bloodless tear.</p>

<p>The Zmey cried in pain, grabbing Jacques by his coat and flinging him through a large glass window, his landing cushioned by the hard, wooden counter and steel shelves of the café.</p><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human">“Uram, jól van?” one of the people hiding inside, voice shaky.</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Wha-? No, I'm fine, let me just-”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human">“Nagyon vérzel! Itt kell maradnod, amíg megérkezik az erősítés.” another said, pushing him to the ground.</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“I said I'm fine!”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human">“Nem hagyhatsz itt minket egyedül, ha-”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Man, I have no idea what you're all saying, and I'm on the clock, so..“</p></blockquote>

<p>Jacques got up, just in time to see the Zmey leering at him through the broken glass, its stance more unbalanced. It puffed, spread its wings, and flew out of sight.</p>

<p>Jacques ran outside, with some of his bones broken and his back full of bleeding wooden splinters, seeing the dragon fly deeper into the city.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“This is all your fault, by the way!“ he screamed at the people still hiding in the destroyed café.</p></blockquote>

<p>But how to catch up to the Zmey? He looked back at the hiding people.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Anyone of you have a still functional car nearby? Car? Auto?"”</p></blockquote>

<p>One man rose, keys in hand.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Bingo.”</p></blockquote>

<p>Jacques drove as fast as the engine could, trying to follow the Zmey scraping against the buildings as it flew past. To his left was his axe, strapped with a safety belt on the passenger seat.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“I messed up, the Zmey is going even deeper into the city.”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“I know. I'll set another cage ahead.”</i> responded the woman.</p></blockquote>

<p>The Zmey landed in a large plaza, with Jacques right behind, guards still trying to to evacuate the area. Jacques had to act now.</p>

<p>He could see the second cage, but how to get the Zmey there? Right now, he had only two choices that didn't risk the safety of others.</p>

<p>But he didn't pick either, as the Zmey picked him in its jaws while he was unaware, thrashing and hurling him into a wall. Thankfully, the hard steel axe on his back eased his impact, the brick-and-mortar wall behind him turned to dust and rubble.</p>

<p>Jacques staggered upright, his head pounding as blood trickled down his face and seeped into his chest. He tried to grab his head, but he realised all too late that his arm was no longer connected to his body, sitting on the ground a few meters away. He fell to his knees, his legs failing him.</p>

<p>Jacques reached behind him, a small Luger-like gun emerging out of the axe's mass. He reached to his belt pouch, yanking it and emptying the insides on the ground.</p>

<p>From the pouch, a mix of liquids spilt out, seeping into the cracked concrete, leaving behind bits of glass and metal. Jacques picked with trembling fingers a single bullet, the red liquid inside glinting in the streetlight. </p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“We only have one regenerator charge left. Your turn.”</p></blockquote>

<p>The axe on his back seemed to whirl in excitement, aware of what was to follow. Jacques lifted the weapon and drove it into his stomach, and with the last of his strength, he twisted the handle. The engine roared to life.</p>

<p>As blood flowed through the axe’s structure, it got hotter, its engine throttling uncontrollably, its entire frame morphing.  Jacques' felt the weapon tearing through his torso, with a mechanical hand emerging out of the metal mass, clawing its way out.</p>
