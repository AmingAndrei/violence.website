<h1 style="text-align:center;font-size:2.5rem;letter-spacing:0.2em;margin:0 0 2rem;">Chapter 1: Peachy</h1>

<p><strong>10.03.10 AW, 07:59, CHROMATICAN CITY</strong></p>

<p>All was quiet. The city was yet to start its day properly. The clock struck 8. The streetlight dimmed ever so slightly as power surged, rising in intensity the second after, simulating the absent daylight. And so, the Chromatican City was officially awake.  </p>

<p>Oh, what a city that was! Obsidian-like skyscrapers filled its horizon. In the centre of the city stood a gigantic spire, haphazardly put together sheets and chunks of metal welded together forming its frame, reaching far above the clouds. The Spine, as it came to be called, a true miracle of engineering based on appearance. The Pale, the city’s governing body, situated itself inside the lower levels of this monolith.</p>

<p>A cacophony of sounds and languages rang across its streets. Every person you walked by was sure to have a unique background and culture. It was a miracle that most of the city could understand each other when speaking.</p>

<p>And so everything ran smoothly, but this was about to change. Radar beeped wildly. Something was heading towards the city, fast! The emergency broadcast warning citizens in the immediate area had barely played before it arrived. The many wandering searchlights near the city’s border and on the rooftops turned to face their target.</p>

<p>The creature landed in the middle of the large boulevard, the concrete cracking under its weight. A large dragon-like being, metal scales shimmering in the light, nearly 30 meters from head to tail. Its roar deafened all who dared be near it, stomping across all that stood in its proximity, stalls smashed through, cars flattened, railings flung across. The surrounding buildings weren’t to be spared either, with the creature not being the most graceful or concerned with its size.</p>

<p>And high above, a helicopter circled the area, surveying the situation. One of its large doors opened, and from within dangled a man, loosely holding himself in with one hand. He reached with the other to a radio strapped to his shoulder, speaking into it.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“I’ve got visuals on the Zmey. How did it even get this close?"</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>"The technician from one of the Signal Stations 'forgot' to tell me about the broken air filter and croaked in his sleep,"</i> responded a woman, tired and monotonous.</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">"That's a shame. I should inform you right now I'm declining the spot, if you're wondering."</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>"Don't worry, Jacques. You're both too valuable for that. And I'd hazard a guess that you can do this on your own?"</i></p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“We’ll handle it all right.”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“Good. Keep me posted.”</i></p></blockquote>

<p>The man, now tasked with taking the Zmey head-on, appeared to be pushing thirty, clean-shaven, hair not particularly styled. The clothes he wore were plain; the only notable piece of clothing being his surprisingly stylish coat, the logo of his handler department, Ultraviolet, displayed proudly on his shoulders. From his belt hung a small pouch, a slight glow emanating from within whenever a ray of light passed over the opening. But most striking was the massive axe strapped to his back, appearing too big to be useful as a weapon. Its appearance had more of a resemblance to what you would find were you to look under a car’s bonnet.</p>

<p>Jacques took a small bottle of a dark-yellow liquid and poured it onto the weapon’s surface, the pilot visibly irritated by the smell of petrol that filled the craft.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Alright, let’s do this!” he muttered, grabbing the axe’s handle and twisting it, trying to rev it up.</p></blockquote>

<p>But nothing happened. He tried again. And again. And again.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Are you for real?”</p></blockquote>

<p>The axe sputtered to life on its own, its metal parts creaking and snapping in sequence. The sounds weren’t random; they were forming a pattern, almost as if it were speaking.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Listen, you're not going to-“</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“Jacques,”</i> the radio crackled again, the woman on the other side much more impatient. <i>“I have direct footage of the situation. Why aren't you down?</i>”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Wait a second! Okay, I promise I won't let it burn you. Alright?”</p></blockquote>

<p>A moment passed, and the axe revved in agreement. Jacques grabbed the handle again, twisting it. The axe roared to life, vibrating as Jacques revved its engine. The helicopter lowered itself just enough for Jacques to leap safely onto a rooftop.</p>

<p>He glanced over at the situation down below. Several people hid inside a café, several hid behind their cars, wherever they could, on such short notice. The Zmey was looking around, investigating its surroundings. It didn’t appear to be actively targeting people, but it was still an animal. If it were to get startled…</p>

<p>He didn’t have any time to waste or to take the lift. Jacques jumped off the rooftop, taking the axe and swinging it, its blade slashing into a wall, slowing his descent just enough not to break his legs. He whistled, drawing the beast's attention.</p>

<p>The beast squinted and roared, batting its wings, trying to make itself seem imposing. Jacques casually approached it. He reached over his shoulder, grabbing the axe's handle, revving the engine inside the mass of metal, roaring in return.</p>

<p>The Zmey flinched at the sound and, startled, in one deep breath, unleashed a stream of yellow fire towards Jacques. He immediately reacted, running behind the rubble and remains caused by the beast’s landing.</p>

<p>Jacques ran toward a maintenance hatch in the street, covering the city’s water lines, looking back to see the weapon molten down. He pulled the axe from over his shoulder and swung it mid-air, its metal plates shifting into the shape of a sword, and plunged it into the ground.</p>

<p>A jet of water burst upward, drenching the air and throwing the Zmey off balance. Jacques darted in close. Whatever drops of water touched the dragon’s scales turned to steam as soon as they made contact.</p>

<p>Jacques ran to the Zmey’s legs, six legs, their appearance more similar to that of an insect than to a reptile. Except for the claws, those were surely reptilian.</p>

<p>Jacques swung the axe. Or rather, the axe swung itself. Jacques was just holding it.</p>

<p>While the creature was still confused and looking for him, he plunged the blade into one of the Zmey’s leg joints. He forced the segments apart until the cartilage gave way with a wet, squelching pop, the joint pulling apart in a clean, bloodless tear.</p>

<p>The Zmey cried in pain, diverting its attention to the man who currently sat near its severed limb. It grabbed Jacques by his coat and lung him through a large glass window, his landing cushioned by the hard, wooden counter and steel shelves of the coffee shop. A few people were hiding inside, ducking behind and under tables.</p><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human">“Jól vagy?” one of them asked, his voice shaky.</p></blockquote><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“No, no, I’m fine,” Jacques groaned, “... I hope you asked that.”</p></blockquote>

<p>Just as he got up, Jacques saw the Zmey leering at him through the broken glass, its stance more unbalanced. It eventually puffed, spread its wings and got off the ground.</p>

<p>Jacques ran outside, with some of his bones broken and his back full of wooden splinters, just in time to see the dragon fly towards the Spine. Jacques reached towards the radio on his shoulder, its plastic covering cracked.</p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“The Zmey’s headed towards the inner city. Evacuate everyone in its general direction!”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“Roger. Support is on the way.”</i> responded the woman.</p></blockquote>

<p>Jacques went back into the coffee shop, trying to tell the people they were safe... and that he needed a car. A person, a Bosnian fellow, eventually tossed him his keys.</p>

<p>Jacques went as fast as the car could, trying to follow the Zmey, which was flying with little regard for its surroundings, the tight space, or the imbalance caused by its missing limb, scraping against the buildings as it flew past. Its leg would likely grow during the next moult cycle, in a few months’ time.</p>

<p>It eventually landed in a large plaza, with Jacques right behind. Uniformed agents were still trying to help and guide the civilians to evacuate the area. Jacques had to act.</p>

<p>But he couldn’t. The plaza was still densely populated, and if he were to get the Zmey’s attention, it would lead to many other people getting hurt. He couldn’t seriously hurt it either, as he couldn’t risk the fire underneath his scales spreading and burning the civilians. Right now, he had two choices.</p>

<p>The Zmey noticed his presence while he was daydreaming of a plan, and it began running towards him. It grabbed him in its jaws, thrashed him to the ground, hurling him into a wall. Thankfully, the hard steel axe on his back eased his impact, with the brick-and-mortar wall behind him cracking and turning to dust and rubble.</p>

<p>Jacques staggered upright, his head pounding as blood trickled down his face and seeped into his chest. He tried to move his hand to his head, but he realised all too late that his arm was no longer connected to his body, sitting on the ground a few meters away. He fell to his knees, his legs ceasing to function.</p>

<p>Jacques reached behind him, from where a small gun was spat out of the axe, a Luger-looking construct. He reached into his belt pouch, yanking it from his belt and emptying the insides on the ground.</p>

<p>From the pouch, a mix of blue and red liquids spilt out, seeping into the cracked concrete, leaving behind bits of glass and metal. Jacques picked with trembling fingers a single bullet, the red liquid inside glinting in the streetlight. He reached towards his radio. </p><blockquote class="left">

<p class="Human">“Impact destroyed almost all Starter charges. I have only one Regenerator left,” he wheezed out. “I’m sending him”</p></blockquote><blockquote class="right">

<p class="Human"><i>“You're asking for permission? Do it already."</i></p></blockquote>

<p>The axe on his back seemed to whirl in excitement, aware of what was to follow. Jacques lifted the weapon and drove it into his stomach, and with the last of his strength, he twisted the handle. The engine roared to life.</p>

<p>As blood flowed through the axe’s structure, it got hotter, its engine throttling uncontrollably, its entire frame shifting around, reaching a level of efficiency unattainable with regular fuel. Jacques felt the plates moving around in his torso before a mechanical hand emerged out of the metal mass, clawing its way out.</p>
