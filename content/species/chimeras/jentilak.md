The Jentil (pl. Jentilak) is a species of large Chimera, native to BLACKSPACE. Noted for their immense size, unique physiology, and peaceful disposition, Jentilak are solitary creatures that inhabit diverse and often extreme environments.

Morphology

Jentilak are humanoid in structure, standing at an average height of approximately 60 metres. They are bipedal, with digitigrade feet and limbs proportionally short yet exceptionally robust, supported by dense musculature and reinforced skeletal structures to sustain their massive frames. Their hands possess four digits, three fingers and one opposable thumb, capable of fine manipulation. The entire body is covered in coarse, brown fur, with a darker mane of softer hair encircling the neck.

The head of a Jentil is distinctively non-humanoid, bearing features that resemble a combination of elephant, aardvark, and anteater. Their facial and nasal regions, lighter in tone, contain two distinct mouths, a standard oral cavity with teeth and tongue, located in the lower facial area, and an elongated, trunk-like upper mouth, functioning both as a nasal extension and a feeding organ, which houses a highly flexible tongue capable of extending up to 30 metres. They have large eyes and small ears located atop the head.

Adaptations

Jentilak are primarily herbivorous, feeding on a wide range of plant life. However, they are classified as opportunistic omnivores, capable of predatory behaviour when necessary.
They posses pores capable of secreting Fael Fire beneath their fur, which facilitates air circulation, allowing for thermal regulation in both extreme cold. Their fur acts as a thermal isolator for both cold and hot climates. They also have specialized glands within their trunk, enabling the mixing of Fael Fire with saliva, which coats their extended tongue with Fael Fire.

Social Behaviour

Jentilak are sexually reproductive, exhibiting no external sexual dimorphism. Reproduction occurs via a cloacal system, and gestation culminates in the birth of live offspring. Neonates are born hairless and remain physically attached to their parent until they develop sufficient fur, at which point they become independent.

Despite their solitary lifestyle, Jentilak are known for their high intelligence, peaceful nature, and cooperative behaviour when interaction occurs. They demonstrate advanced problem-solving, communication, and environmental awareness, although they rarely form long-term social groups.
