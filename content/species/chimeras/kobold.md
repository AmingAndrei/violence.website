The Kobold (pl. Kobolds) is a bipedal species of Chimera, native to BLACKSPACE. These entities are distinguished by their towering stature, unique biological integration with gold, and characteristic behaviours surrounding hoarding and territorial isolation. Highly durable and extremely solitary, Kobolds are recognized both for their formidable physical attributes and their obsessive compulsion toward gold accumulation.

Morphology and Physiology

Kobolds exhibit a humanoid body plan, standing approximately 4 meters in height, with a lanky frame, elongated limbs, and an exceptionally long neck. The body is covered in thin black scales, while the muscular and skeletal structures are highly dense and durable, enabling a Kobold to overpower an adult human with a single limb.
- Hands are tridactyl, featuring two long, flexible fingers and a thumb.
- Legs are digitigrade, adapted for agile, powerful movement.
- Head is covered in a metallic dermal layer composed primarily of gold, giving the face a solid golden appearance. The head bears two massive ears, three backward-facing horns, lacks a nasal structure, features sharp dentition, and eyes with a crystalline sheen, making closing their eyes unnecessary, lacking eyelids altogether.

Gold Dependency and Fael Fire Integration

Kobolds exhibit a biological dependency on gold, which is essential to their metabolic systems. They actively consume gold as a nutrient, and use grey Fael Fire, secreted through dermal pores, to melt and bind gold to their scales, forming an adaptive, flexible armour.
- Located within their cheeks are additional Fael Fire sacs, maintain elevated temperatures in the cranial region and allow for oral emission of Fael Fire in small, low-efficiency bursts.
- The head remains perpetually heated due to internal Fael Fire activity, a trait tied to both defence and metabolic necessity, in order to ease the processing of gold.

Behaviour and Psychology

Kobolds are kleptomaniacal, asocial, and display behavioural patterns akin to Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder (OCD). They form individual dens, which function as hoards for gold materials, used for sustenance, protection, and psychological gratification.
- Kobolds are capable of expressive facial gestures, including wide grins when elated or distress over missing gold.
- Kobolds obsessively monitor their collections using their highly developed visual memory, capable of detecting minute changes or missing items.
- Their arms are often encased in numerous gold rings and bracelets, serving both decorative and defensive purposes.
- They possess a refined sense of taste capable of detecting trace gold in soil. Their strength and Fael Fire enable them to engage in basic mining and smithing activities. They may additionally steal any gold item they stumble upon, and show visible disgust when presented with forgeries.

Diet and Reproduction

Outside of their gold intake, Kobolds are strict herbivores. Reproduction is sexual, with no observable dimorphism between sexes.
- Females produce one egg per clutch, depositing it within their former dens along with stockpiled food and gold.
- Hatchlings are entirely black in coloration at birth; the golden facial structure develops later, concurrent with the onset of Fael Fire production and physical maturity.
- Kobolds typically reproduce two to three times over the course of their lifespan.
