**Valences** are a sentient species of **Mosaic**, originating withing **BLACKSPACE**. They represent a unique subclass of **Mosaics**, distinguished by their symbiotic interaction with **external mechanical constructs**, referred to as _shells_, which serve as both exoskeleton and interface.

**Physiological Composition**

The true biological form of a Valence, colloquially termed its **heart**, is a compact Voxel entity:
- **Average length** of ~30 cm
- Resembles various terrestrial invertebrates, particularly slugs or crustaceans, capable of limited locomotion
- Their **surface texture** is jagged, crystalline, often described as **yellow-tinted glass**
- Includes rudimentary **analogue-camera-like eyes**, and **basic auditory communication systems** akin to simplified speakers and microphones

Despite their relatively primitive individual form, the heart is the central processing unit of the Valence, responsible for all higher cognitive and motor control functions.

**Shell**  
To overcome the limitations of their vulnerable primary form, Valences utilize advanced **humanoid mechanical shells**, into which they interface via the chest cavity. Upon entry:
- The heart disperses its **Voxels** throughout the chassis, establishing complete **motor and sensory control**.
- These shells are typically between **1.9 and 2 metres** in height and constructed from durable **white-metal alloys**, with signature **yellow LED eyes** serving as primary visual interfaces, with the advanced visual and auditory sensors far surpassing the baseline heart.
- Modular components allow for shell upgrades and repairs.

Once linked, the shell effectively functions as an extension of the Valence’s body and nervous system.

**Vulnerabilities**

Valences, while formidable within their shells, exhibit **significant biological and operational weaknesses**:
- The heart remains the single point of failure. Although it is housed within the chest cavity of the shell, a **direct strike** to the torso can **fatally compromise the organism**.
- **Trauma to the shell** can **transfer partial damage** to the internal organism. This makes indirect attacks (e.g., blunt force or high-impact collisions) potentially lethal over time.
- **Decapitation or Neural Severance**: In many cases, decapitating a Valence shell does not kill the entity, but **renders it catatonic**, due to the severance of primary sensory and motor control conduits.
