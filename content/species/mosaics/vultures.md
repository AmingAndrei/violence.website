Vultures are a hybridized entity resulting from the possession of a deceased or dying <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span> by a <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span>. This event leads to a parasitic fusion wherein the Vesper subsumes and reanimates the <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span>'s body.

Formation

The creation of a Vulture occurs when a <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span> infiltrates the body of a compromised <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span>, typically one that is either biologically deceased or neurologically nonviable. Upon entry, the <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span> initiates a full-body integration, overtaking both the shell and remaining systems of the <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span>. This integration is not a simple reanimation; it is a full fusion, blending the Voxel structure of the <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span> with that of the <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span> host.

Morphology

Vultures retain a high degree of anatomical similarity to the host <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span>, including preservation of the shell. However, Vultures are distinguishable by the following traits:
- Anomalous extremities or appendages that mirror the morphology of the possessing <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span>
- Modified facial or cranial structures

Cognitive Profile

Psychologically, Vultures retain the full consciousness, memories, and behavioural patterning of the <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span>. However, remnants of the original <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span> neurological structure may persist, resulting in:
- Partial memories of the <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span> host
- Intermittent behavioural conflict between the identities of the <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span> and <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span>
- Occasional hybrid cognition, wherein decisions, expressions, or emotional responses may reflect the influence of the <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span>, though control remains <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vesper</Span>-dominant

These effects vary based on the degree of psychological degradation prior to possession.

Capabilities

As derivatives of <span style="color:#FD3DB5">Vespers</Span>, Vultures exhibit numerous advanced capabilities, including the usage of Vectors, and, in cases where the <span style="color:#FFDE21">Valence</Span> host was previously a <span style="color:#FFDE21">Vexer</Span>, access to multiple Vectors.
