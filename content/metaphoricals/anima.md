Anima are a metaphoric phenomena and autonomous entities that randomly manifest and form temporary, involuntary connections with individual hosts. Their emergence and disappearance follow no discernible pattern.

Appearance

Anima manifest as white, levitating objects measuring approximately 8 centimetres in length, with a form resembling an inverted heart symbol. From their central body, a series of filamentous tendrils or “strings” extend and encircle the host’s neck in a loose, non-restrictive configuration. These strings serve as both tether and interface, forming the only known connection between the Anima and the host.

While active, the Anima remains constantly visible, emits a faint self-illumination, and is capable of limited hovering movement within the radius permitted by the tethering strings. Attempts to sever the strings or destroy the Anima have been uniformly unsuccessful; the entity is considered indestructible.

Influence

Upon manifestation, the Anima gains partial physical control over the host. While hosts retain full consciousness and sensory awareness, they report a diminished capacity to control motor functions, particularly in high-stress or reactive scenarios. Despite this, hosts consistently exhibit improved reflexes, motor coordination, and task-specific proficiency during periods of Anima control.

In addition to physical enhancement, Anima hosts demonstrate access to information and skills that should be inaccessible or unknowable. Reports include cases of precognition, linguistic fluency, and intuitive technical knowledge.

Anima also appear to confer a form of quantum immortality, forcibly preserving host continuity across potential fatal events through localised temporal manipulation and probabilistic denial of death. This effect is not absolute, but renders conventional lethality inconsistent and unreliable.

Communication

Anima do not communicate verbally. Instead, they exert influence through, tactile cues, such as tugging on strings to gain attention, manipulative behaviours, including compelling the host to observe or approach objects or persons, and subtle coercions aimed at directing the host toward specific goals or actions, which remain undisclosed or only partially understood by the host. Despite their influence, Anima do not appear to exert complete dominance. Hosts can resist or question Anima control.

Termination

Anima connections can cease abruptly and without warning, resulting in loss of all associated enhancements, reinstatement of full host autonomy. The mechanism behind Anima termination remains unknown.
