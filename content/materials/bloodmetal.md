**Bloodmetal** is a crystalline-metallic substance native to Blackspace. It is visually identifiable by its **deep red coloration**, ranging from translucent crimson to opaque maroon, which gives the material its common designation. Despite its metallic luster and superficial resemblance to hematite or iron, Bloodmetal exhibits **structural characteristics more consistent with diamond-like carbon** materials.

**Physical Properties**

- **Color**: Varies across red hues; highly refractive.
- **Structure**: Crystalline; lattice arrangement akin to carbon-based alloys.
- **Density**: Slightly lower than elemental silver, contributing to a favorable strength-to-weight ratio.
- **Hardness**: Approximates that of high-grade industrial ceramics; can be honed to sharpness comparable to obsidian or diamond tools.

**Composition and Origin**

Analytical testing has revealed that Bloodmetal is **composed primarily of coagulated blood**, including:
- **Hemoglobin-rich organic compounds** from multiple species.
- **Preserved DNA strands**, often of human or humanoid origin.
    - Some DNA sequences match **known individuals**.
    - Others correspond to **non-existent or anomalous profiles**, including those of undocumented individuals or species.

**Formation and Synthesis**

**Natural Formation**:
- Believed to result from **spontaneous or environmental Blackstone shifts**, particularly in proximity to biological remains or concentrated Imago emissions.

**Artificial Synthesis**:

- Can be manufactured via:
    - **Mechanical synthesis**: Using high-pressure carbon infusion systems and raw blood samples.
    - **Biological secretion**: By organisms capable of processing blood and carbon into crystalline output.

 Synthesis requires a **free carbon source** to stabilize the density and crystallization phase.
