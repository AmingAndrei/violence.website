**Voltite** is an anomalous, metallic substance found exclusively within Blackspace. It is characterized by its **pale yellow hue**, **metallic sheen**, and **notable malleability**, lending itself readily to structural manipulation and technological integration.

**Physical and Chemical Properties**

- **Appearance**: Pale yellow metal with a soft metallic lustre.
- **Density**: Comparable to aluminium (~2.7 g/cm³), making it significantly lighter than most industrial metals.
- **Melting Point**: Approximately 2000°C.
- **Mechanical Properties**: High flexibility and resilience under stress, with no known fatigue degradation under repeated strain.

**Electrical and Thermal Characteristics**

Voltite’s most defining feature is its **exceptional electrical conductivity**, which has been measured at levels **up to 100 times greater than that of traditional conductors** such as gold or copper.

In contrast to its electrical behaviour, Voltite demonstrates **extreme resistance to heat transfer**, effectively functioning as a **thermal insulator**. This decoupling of electrical and thermal conductivities is considered anomalous by Realspace physics standards and is presently unexplained.

- **Electrical Conductivity**: Superconductive

- **Thermal Conductivity**: Negligible

This disparity permits Voltite-based systems to **operate under sustained high-voltage conditions** without overheating or requiring external cooling measures.
