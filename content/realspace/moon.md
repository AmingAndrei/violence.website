The **Moon** is Earth's only natural satellite, historically classified as a satellite planet orbiting the primary planetary body of Earth. Following the events of the Ashen Winter Incident, the Moon underwent severe and anomalous structural, physical, and temporal alterations.

**Structural Collapse and Hollowing**

Post-AWI analysis indicates that the **Moon experienced catastrophic structural failure**, with its **upper mantle fractured** into numerous large fissures and **several surface segments missing entirely**. These **exposed voids** allow direct observation into the lunar interior, revealing that:
- The **core appears to have collapsed inward**, suggesting either a **massive internal detonation**, gravitational anomaly, or extradimensional interference.
- Geological assessments of the interior walls show **signs of advanced rock erosion**, impact trauma, and stress fractures.
- The Moon, once solid, is now believed to be a **mostly hollow shell**, lacking both its original mass and density.

**Environmental Impact on Earth**

The Moon's destabilization was directly linked to multiple **planetary-scale consequences** during and after the AWI:
- Fine particulates expelled from the fractured Moon entered Earth’s atmosphere, contributing significantly to **atmospheric occlusion** and plant die-off.
- **Mass redistribution** altered lunar gravitation, triggering irregular **tidal surges, short-term tsunamis**, and contributing to **long-term flooding** in coastal and low-lying regions.
- **Disruption of global air and ocean currents** followed the destabilization, with many systems collapsing entirely or shifting unpredictably.

**Anomalous Signal Emissions**

Since the AWI, the Moon has become a **source of irregular, non-linear signal emissions**, currently classified as **temporal anomalies**. These signals exhibit the following traits:
- **Non-chronological transmission**, with signal data referencing events in both the **Golden Autumn** and **Ashen Winter** eras without consistent order.
- Leading hypotheses propose that the Moon now serves as a **link** between the **pre-AWI timeline** and the current era, potentially solving the **temporal gap** observed in Realspace chronometry.
